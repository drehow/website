Stock markets behave in opposite ways depending on whether you’re perspective is micro- or macroscopic. It’s easy to forecast that our economy will be bigger in the distant future than it is now, but difficult to estimate where it will be in a month. As a result, it’s harder to trade profitable options than it is to make profitable long-term trades in index funds. This article explores the different levels of randomness you get from highly observed stock indexes depending on the length of time under scrutiny.
